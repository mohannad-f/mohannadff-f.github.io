English Translation & Commentary

"Abdullah ibn Amr (may Allah be pleased with him) said: 'The Prophet (peace be upon him) was not indecent nor did he speak indecently, and he used to say: Verily, among the best of you are those with the most excellent character.'" [Bukhari 3559]

Dear reader, this hadith opens a magnificent door to understanding the character of the best of humanity ๏ทบ. It first describes him by negating what is unbecoming: "was not indecent" - meaning there was nothing in his speech or actions that would offend modesty or wound hearts. An indecent person deliberately uses foul language or vulgar behavior, but the Prophet ๏ทบ was far above that. Every word that came from his noble mouth was pure, clean, like fresh water to a thirsty heart.

Then it says: "nor did he speak indecently" - meaning he didn't even force himself to be crude or pretend to be so. Some people may not naturally be indecent, but when they get angry or want to show toughness, they adopt foul language. But the Beloved ๏ทบ was pure in his inner and outer self, in his pleasure and anger, in his private and public life. Anger only increased his dignity, and never took him out of the circle of forbearance.

Then we hear the jewel of the hadith: "Verily, among the best of you are those with the most excellent character." Here the Prophet ๏ทบ raises the standard by which people are measured. He didn't say: the richest among you, or the strongest, or those with the most authority. Instead, he made the scale of goodness and virtue to be excellent character. It's as if the Messenger ๏ทบ is telling us: the path to closeness to him, and the path to Allah's love, is not through abundance of wealth or power of status, but through gentleness of heart, restraining anger, showing mercy, truthfulness, cheerfulness, and loyalty.

If you reflect, dear reader, you'll feel that this hadith is like a clear mirror, reflecting our true image. When you read "among the best of you are those with the most excellent character," you find yourself asking: Where do I stand by this measure? Am I among the best of people? How is my character with my family, my parents, my friends, those I disagree with?

The Prophet ๏ทบ was a role model in every good character trait. He would smile in the faces of his companions, until Jarir ibn Abdullah said: "The Messenger of Allah ๏ทบ never saw me except that he smiled in my face." He was merciful to children, carrying them and playing with them. He was forbearing with the ignorant, forgiving and pardoning even those who wronged him. He was faithful to his promises, truthful in his speech, gentle in his social interactions.

This hadith is not merely a description of the Prophet ๏ทบ, but a practical invitation for us to follow in his footsteps. Good character is not a cosmetic or secondary matter, but the essence and spirit of religion. As came in another hadith: "Nothing is heavier in the believer's scale on the Day of Judgment than good character."

Good character makes your life and the lives of those around you a small paradise before the paradise of the Hereafter. Imagine if every person treated others with gentleness, mercy, and kind words - how souls would find peace and hearts would become pure.

In summary, dear reader, this hadith reminds you to be like the Prophet ๏ทบ: pure in your speech, gentle in your dealings, truthful in your character. The more you perfect your character, the closer you get to being among the best of people, and indeed closer to the Messenger of Allah ๏ทบ in rank, for he said: "The closest of you to me in seating on the Day of Judgment will be those with the most excellent character."
